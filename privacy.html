Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit and can enhance your browsing experience. Cookies may be either "persistent" cookies or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device after you close your browser, while session cookies are deleted when you close your browser.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our website
  • Improve the functionality and performance of our website
  • Deliver targeted advertising
  • Prevent fraud and enhance security
  • Analyze website traffic and user behavior

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled.
  • Performance Cookies: These cookies collect information about how you use our website, such as which pages you visit and any errors you encounter.
  • Functional Cookies: These cookies allow us to remember your preferences and provide enhanced functionality.
  • Targeting Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver advertising that is relevant to you and your interests.

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can set your browser to refuse all cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. However, some features of our website may not function properly if you disable cookies. For more information about managing cookies, please visit your browser's help section or www.allaboutcookies.org.

Other Tracking Technologies

We may also use other tracking technologies, such as web beacons and pixels, to collect information about your browsing activities and to remember your preferences. These technologies may be used in conjunction with cookies to track your activity across different websites.
